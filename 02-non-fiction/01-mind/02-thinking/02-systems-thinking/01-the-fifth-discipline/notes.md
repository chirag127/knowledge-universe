## Author Background

Peter Senge (b. 1947) is a senior lecturer at the MIT Sloan
School of Management. He founded the Society for
Organizational Learning (SoL) and is a pioneer of systems
thinking in management. He studied under Jay Forrester
(system dynamics) and was influenced by Chris Argyris and
David Bohm.

## Personal Notes

- The beer game (originally developed at MIT by Forrester and
Sterman) is used throughout the book as a teaching tool for
systems thinking.
- Senge draws heavily on the system dynamics tradition from
MIT's Jay Forrester.
- Mental Models chapter relies on Chris Argyris's theories of
action science and defensive routines.
- The 2006 edition adds ~100 pages of new material based on
15 years of practitioner experience.
- Originally published by Currency/Doubleday; has been
translated into 30+ languages.
- HBR named it one of the seminal management books of the
past 75 years in 1997.
