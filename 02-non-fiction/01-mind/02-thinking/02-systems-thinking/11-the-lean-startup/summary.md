## The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Startups are not smaller versions of large companies — they
are temporary organizations designed to operate under
conditions of extreme uncertainty. The Lean Startup applies
scientific experimentation to entrepreneurship: instead of
building elaborate plans based on untested assumptions,
entrepreneurs should build minimum viable products (MVPs),
measure customer response, and learn whether to pivot or
persevere. This Build-Measure-Learn feedback loop replaces
traditional business planning with validated learning,
radically reducing waste and increasing the odds of success.
