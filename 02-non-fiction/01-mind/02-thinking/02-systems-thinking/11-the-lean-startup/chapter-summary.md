# The Lean Startup — Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

## Reading Guidance

Part I (Chapters 1-3) introduces the vision and core
concepts. Part II (Chapters 4-8) is the heart — the Build-
Measure-Learn framework. Part III (Chapters 9-14) covers
acceleration and scaling.

---

### Introduction

**Core Question:** Why do most startups fail?

**What it Covers:** The high failure rate of startups. The
Lean Startup as a management methodology. Contrast with
traditional business planning.

**Key Concepts:** Startup, uncertainty, entrepreneurial
management.

> **_A startup is a human institution designed to create a
new product or service under conditions of extreme
uncertainty._**

---

### Part I: Vision

---

### Chapter 1: Start

**Core Question:** What is a startup?

**What it Covers:** Definition of a startup. The startup as
an experiment machine. The fallacy of the "just do it"
approach.

**Key Concepts:** Startup definition, experimental
approach, scientific method.

---

### Chapter 2: Define

**Core Question:** Who is the entrepreneur?

**What it Covers:** Entrepreneurs are everywhere — not just
in garages. The importance of entrepreneurial management
in any organization facing uncertainty.

**Key Concepts:** Entrepreneur, intrapreneurship,
management in uncertainty.

---

### Chapter 3: Learn

**Core Question:** What is validated learning?

**What it Covers:** Learning as the essential unit of
progress. The failure of traditional metrics. Examples
of companies that learned their way to success.

**Key Concepts:** Validated learning, learning milestones,
false positives.

> **_Validated learning is the process of demonstrating
empirically that a team has discovered valuable truths
about a startup's present and future prospects._**

---

### Part II: Steer

---

### Chapter 4: Experiment

**Core Question:** How do you test assumptions?

**What it Covers:** Startups as experiments. The leap-of-
faith assumptions: value hypothesis and growth hypothesis.
The MVP approach.

**Key Concepts:** Value hypothesis, growth hypothesis,
experiment design, leap-of-faith.

---

### Chapter 5: Leap

**Core Question:** Where do you start building?

**What it Covers:** The minimum viable product (MVP).
Determining what to build first. The tension between
quality and speed.

**Key Concepts:** MVP, minimum viable product, quality-
speed tradeoff.

---

### Chapter 6: Test

**Core Question:** How do you measure progress?

**What it Covers:** Innovation accounting — the three
learning milestones. Establishing the baseline,
tuning the engine, pivot or persevere.

**Key Concepts:** Innovation accounting, baseline,
tuning, pivots.

> **_Innovation accounting enables startups to prove
objectively that they are learning how to grow a
sustainable business._**

---

### Chapter 7: Measure

**Core Question:** What metrics matter?

**What it Covers:** Vanity metrics vs. actionable metrics.
Split-test experiments. Cohort analysis. The difference
between leading and lagging indicators.

**Key Concepts:** Vanity metrics, actionable metrics,
split-test, cohort analysis.

---

### Chapter 8: Pivot (or Persevere)

**Core Question:** When should you change direction?

**What it Covers:** The pivot — a structured course
correction. Types of pivots (zoom-in, zoom-out, customer
segment, platform, etc.). The pivot-or-persevere meeting.

**Key Concepts:** Pivot, persevere, pivot types, runways.

---

### Part III: Accelerate

---

### Chapter 9: Batch

**Core Question:** How small can you make batches?

**What it Covers:** Small batch sizes reduce cycle time.
The Toyota Production System influence. Continuous
deployment in software.

**Key Concepts:** Batch size, small batches, continuous
deployment, pull vs. push.

---

### Chapter 10: Grow

**Core Question:** How do startups grow?

**What it Covers:** The three engines of growth: sticky,
viral, and paid. Sustainable growth. Choosing the right
engine.

**Key Concepts:** Sticky engine, viral engine, paid
engine, sustainable growth.

---

### Chapter 11: Adapt

**Core Question:** How do you maintain speed as you grow?

**What it Covers:** The Five Whys technique for root cause
analysis. Building an adaptive organization. The tradeoff
between speed and quality.

**Key Concepts:** Five Whys, root cause, adaptive
organization, speed-quality tradeoff.

---

### Chapter 12: Innovate

**Core Question:** How do established companies innovate?

**What it Covers:** The innovator's dilemma. Creating
innovation sanctuaries. Three structures for innovation:
teams, labs, and internal startups.

**Key Concepts:** Innovator's dilemma, innovation
sanctuary, intrapreneurship.

---

### Chapter 13: Epilogue

**What it Covers:** Summary and call to action. The Lean
Startup movement. Implications for the future of business.

---

### Chapter 14: The Long Now (Notable)

**Key Concepts:** Long-term thinking, sustainability.
