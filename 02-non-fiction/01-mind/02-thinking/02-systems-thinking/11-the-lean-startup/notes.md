## Author Background

Eric Ries (b. 1978) is an American entrepreneur, author,
and blogger. He co-founded IMVU, a social networking
service, in 2004, where he developed the Lean Startup
methodology in practice. He was an entrepreneur-in-residence
at Harvard Business School and has advised numerous startups
and Fortune 500 companies. His blog, Startup Lessons
Learned, became the foundation for the book. He also wrote
*The Startup Way* (2017). The Lean Startup has sold over
one million copies worldwide.

## Personal Notes

- The Lean Startup adapts principles from lean manufacturing
(Toyota Production System) to entrepreneurship —
specifically the concepts of batch size reduction, pull
vs. push, and continuous improvement.
- The Build-Measure-Learn loop is essentially a cybernetic
feedback loop — it applies the same logic as the
thermostat's sense-respond cycle.
- "Innovation accounting" is the most underappreciated
concept — most startups fail because they use traditional
financial accounting which is useless for early-stage
ventures.
- The "pivot" concept has been criticized as being used
retrospectively to frame failures as strategic decisions.
- The book is more applicable to B2C/software startups than
to hardware, biotech, or capital-intensive ventures (Ries
acknowledges this).
- The Lean Startup has been influential beyond startups —
governments, large enterprises, and non-profits use it.
- Steve Blank's "Customer Development" model is the
intellectual predecessor — Ries systematized and
popularized it.
