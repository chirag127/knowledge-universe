## Author Background

John Gall (1925-2014) was a pediatrician and systems
theorist. His medical background informed his observations
about systemic failure in hospitals and large organizations.
He was not an academic systems theorist but a practicing
physician who documented system behavior with surgical wit.

## Personal Notes

- Gall's Law is the most cited concept from the book and is
often invoked in software engineering (particularly agile
and lean movements).
- The book is written in the style of a scientific textbook
but is actually satire — Gall's own "Law of the
Irreproducible Result" being a case in point.
- The 2002 reissue was significantly expanded and retitled
"The Systems Bible."
- Many of Gall's observations predate and anticipate
concepts in Senge, Dorner, and Perrow.
- The read feels remarkably fresh — Gall was writing about
system failure patterns decades before they became
mainstream.
