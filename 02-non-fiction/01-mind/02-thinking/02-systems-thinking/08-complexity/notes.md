## Author Background

Melanie Mitchell (b. 1958) is a computer scientist,
cognitive scientist, and professor at the Santa Fe Institute
(formerly) and Portland State University. She earned her
Ph.D. from the University of Michigan under John Holland
(the inventor of genetic algorithms). She worked at the
Santa Fe Institute for many years and is a leading figure in
complex systems, artificial intelligence, and cognitive
science. Her other books include *An Introduction to Genetic
Algorithms* (1999) and *Artificial Intelligence: A Guide for
Thinking Humans* (2019).

## Personal Notes

- The book is based on Mitchell's course "Introduction to
Complexity" taught at the Santa Fe Institute's Complex
Systems Summer School.
- She was a direct student of John Holland, so the Genetic
Algorithms chapter has special authority.
- The book was awarded the Phi Beta Kappa Book Award in
Science — rare recognition for a popular science book.
- The NK model chapter is particularly valuable for
understanding the theoretical foundations of complexity.
- One gap: the book is relatively light on economic
complexity (e.g., Arthur's increasing returns, complex
adaptive economic systems).
- A strength: Mitchell is extremely honest about what
complexity science has NOT yet accomplished — open problems
are clearly stated.
