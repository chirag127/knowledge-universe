## Author Background

Donella "Dana" Meadows (1941-2001) was a pioneering
environmental scientist, systems analyst, and lead author of
the seminal 1972 book *The Limits to Growth*. She earned a
Ph.D. in system dynamics from MIT, where she studied under
Jay Forrester. She was a professor at Dartmouth College and
co-founded the Sustainability Institute. Her weekly column
"The Global Citizen" was syndicated internationally. She
was awarded a MacArthur Fellowship (1994).

## Personal Notes

- The manuscript was largely complete at the time of
Meadows's death in 2001. Diana Wright spent 7 years
preparing it for publication.
- The leverage points chapter originated as a 1997 article
published in *Whole Earth* magazine.
- Meadows was a student of Jay Forrester (the inventor of
system dynamics) and a mentor to Peter Senge.
- The book intentionally avoids computer models and
mathematics, focusing on conceptual understanding.
- The bathtub metaphor for stocks and flows is arguably the
most famous teaching tool in systems thinking.
- Recommended reading order: Chapter 1 and 2 first, then
Chapter 5-7, then 3-4.
