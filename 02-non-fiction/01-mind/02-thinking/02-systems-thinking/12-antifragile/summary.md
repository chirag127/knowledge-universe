## The Big Idea in One Paragraph

There are three categories of response to stress: fragile
(broken by shocks), robust (unaffected), and antifragile
(which gain from shocks, volatility, and disorder). Taleb
argues that complex systems naturally require stressors to
remain healthy — the removal of all volatility (via
over-optimization, central planning, and intervention)
makes systems fragile and prone to catastrophic collapse.
The antidote is to build antifragility: small-scale
decentralization, redundancy, optionality, and skin in the
game. Less is often more; the fragile seek comfort while
the antifragile seek stress.

## Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### 1. The Triad
The classification of everything into Fragile, Robust, and
Antifragile. The central organizing framework of the book.
- **Chapters**: 1-4

### 2. The Via Negativa
What you do not do matters as much as what you do.
Subtraction, not addition. Removing fragilizers.
- **Chapters**: 5-8

### 3. Optionality & Convexity
Antifragility is linked to convexity — benefiting from
variation. Optionality is the key. Barbell strategy.
- **Chapters**: 9-13

### 4. The Ethics of Antifragility
Skin in the game. The asymmetry of risk. The difference
between those who take risk and those who transfer it.
- **Chapters**: 14-18
