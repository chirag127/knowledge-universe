## Author Background

Steven Strogatz (b. 1959) is a renowned mathematician and
professor at Cornell University. He earned his Ph.D. from
Harvard (1986) and was a Rhodes Scholar at Oxford. He is a
leading figure in nonlinear dynamics and network theory. He
co-created the Watts-Strogatz model of small-world networks
with Duncan Watts (published in Nature, 1998). He is also
the author of *Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos* (1994), the
standard graduate textbook on the subject, and *The Joy of
X* (2012), a popular book on mathematics.

## Personal Notes

- Strogatz's discovery with Duncan Watts was made while
Watts was his Ph.D. student — a rare student-outshines-
advisor story.
- The small-world network paper (Watts & Strogatz, Nature
1998) has over 50,000 citations — a genuine landmark.
- The Kuramoto model was proposed by Yoshiki Kuramoto in
1975 and remains the canonical model for studying
synchronization.
- Sync is one of those rare books that genuinely bridges
mathematics, biology, and human experience.
- The "dark side of sync" chapter (Millennium Bridge,
epilepsy) is particularly compelling.
- A weakness: the book could spend more time on the
mathematical details for those who want them. Readers who
want the equations should consult Strogatz's textbook.
