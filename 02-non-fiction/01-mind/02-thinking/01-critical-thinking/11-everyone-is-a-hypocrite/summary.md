## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

The reason everyone else is a hypocrite — and you are too, whether you realize it or not — is that the human mind is not a single, unified decision-maker but a collection of specialized modules that evolved to solve different adaptive problems. Each module operates with its own goals, values, and information, and they do not always (or even often) communicate with each other. This modular design explains why people simultaneously hold contradictory beliefs, preach one thing and do another, deceive themselves about their own motives, and honestly believe they are consistent while being transparently hypocritical to others. There is no central "self" that could be consistent; there is only a coalition of competing neural systems. Kurzban argues that understanding this design — rather than condemning hypocrisy — is the only path to genuinely understanding human behavior.
