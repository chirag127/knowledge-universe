### Author

**Dan Ariely** — James B. Duke Professor of Behavioral Economics at Duke University. Founder of the Center for Advanced Hindsight. His research focuses on the irrational forces that shape human decision-making. Before Duke, he held positions at MIT (MIT Media Lab and Sloan School of Management).

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Key Takeaways
- The decoy effect is everywhere in marketing and pricing
- FREE! is a uniquely powerful force that short-circuits rational analysis
- Social norms and market norms should never be mixed
- We cannot predict how we'll behave in emotional states (hot/cold gap)
- Ownership fundamentally changes our valuation of things
- Precommitment is the most reliable tool against procrastination
- Price affects not just choice but actual experienced efficacy

### Strengths
- Extraordinary experiments that are both clever and memorable
- Conversational, funny, deeply engaging writing style
- Each chapter stands alone as a complete exploration
- The experiments are replicable and well-documented

### Limitations
- More descriptive than prescriptive — fewer solutions than diagnoses
- Some experiments have been criticized for small sample sizes
- The revised edition is somewhat disjointed (patched together)
- Over-reliance on laboratory experiments may not fully generalize
- Less applicable to organizational or group decision-making

### Legacy
- One of the most widely read popular behavioral economics books
- Helped popularize the field for a general audience
- Frequently cited in marketing, product design, and policy
- Ariely's TED talks (based on the book) have millions of views
