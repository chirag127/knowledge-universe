## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Most decision-making research studies novices in artificial lab settings, concluding that people are biased and irrational. But when you study experts making real decisions under pressure — firefighters, pilots, nurses, military commanders — you discover that they don't compare options analytically. Instead, they rely on experience-driven intuition: they recognize patterns, simulate scenarios mentally, and make rapid decisions based on what has worked before. The true "sources of power" in decision-making are not rational analysis but recognition, mental simulation, storytelling, metaphors, and team cognition.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### 1. The Recognition-Primed Decision (RPD) Model
Experts don't compare options. They recognize a situation as familiar and immediately know the appropriate course of action. The RPD model has three variants: simple match, diagnose the situation, and evaluate course of action through mental simulation.

### 2. Naturalistic Decision Making (NDM)
The study of how people make decisions in real-world settings with time pressure, high stakes, incomplete information, and shifting conditions — as opposed to artificial lab experiments.

### 3. Intuition as Pattern Recognition
Intuition is not magical — it's the rapid, unconscious matching of current situations to patterns learned through experience.

### 4. Mental Simulation
The ability to imagine how a situation unfolded (explanation) or will unfold (projection). Critical for sense-making and decision evaluation.

### 5. Storytelling as Sense-Making
Stories are not just entertainment — they are how we understand complex situations, transmit expertise, and build shared mental models.

### 6. Team Mind
Effective teams develop a shared mental model — a "team mind" — that allows coordinated action without explicit communication.

### 7. The Limits of Rational Analysis ("Hyperrationality")
Over-reliance on analytical decision tools can be dangerous — it ignores the power of experience and intuition.
