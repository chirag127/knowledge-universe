## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Leaders systematically fail to notice critical information that is available but not salient — a condition Bazerman calls "bounded awareness." We miss data that doesn't fit our mental models, fail to see unethical behavior that unfolds gradually, overlook what didn't happen, and accept too-good-to-be-true scenarios without sufficient scrutiny. The best leaders train themselves to notice what others miss by asking better questions, looking for missing information, anticipating future scenarios, and recognizing their own blind spots.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### 1. Bounded Awareness
The systematic failure to notice important information that is observable but not salient. Leaders must deliberately expand their awareness beyond natural starting points.

### 2. Motivated Blindness
We fail to notice unethical behavior when noticing would conflict with our self-interest. This explains why auditors miss client fraud, why leaders overlook misconduct.

### 3. The Slippery Slope
Unethical behavior is more likely to be missed when it occurs incrementally — each step is small enough to be rationalized until the overall change is enormous.

### 4. The Dog That Didn't Bark
Learning from what didn't happen (missing information) is as important as noticing what did happen. The absence of evidence is itself evidence.

### 5. Thinking Ahead
Anticipating future scenarios and predictable surprises. The best leaders don't just react — they forecast and prepare.

### 6. Indirect vs. Direct Harm
We systematically care less about harm that is indirect (via third parties, systems, or chains of causation). This leads to predictable ethical blind spots.

### 7. Developing the Capacity to Notice
Specific strategies: zooming out, asking "what's missing?", considering the opposite, creating organizational structures that surface hidden information.

|------------|-------------|------------|
| **Bounded Awareness** | Focusing on available info only | Deliberately seek what's missing |
| **Motivated Blindness** | Not seeing what conflicts with self-interest | Identify conflicts in advance |
| **Slippery Slope** | Missing incremental ethical erosion | Set clear thresholds and boundaries |
| **Missing Data** | Not noticing what didn't happen | Ask "what's not being said?" |
| **Too-Good-To-Be-True** | Accepting implausible claims | Scrutinize anomalies aggressively |
| **Short-Term Focus** | Not anticipating long-term risks | Use pre-mortems and scenario planning |
| **Indirect Harm** | Caring less about distant harm | Trace full chains of causation |
| **Predictable Surprises** | Failing to act on known risks | Create systems for early warning |
