### Authors

**John S. Hammond** — Consultant on decision analysis and former professor at Harvard Business School and MIT Sloan School of Management.

**Ralph L. Keeney** — Professor Emeritus at Duke University's Fuqua School of Business. A pioneer in the field of decision analysis and value-focused thinking. Co-authored *Decisions with Multiple Objectives* with Raiffa.

**Howard Raiffa** — Frank Plumpton Ramsey Professor of Managerial Economics (Emeritus) at Harvard Business School and Harvard Kennedy School. A founding father of decision analysis, game theory, and negotiation analysis. Co-authored *The Art and Science of Negotiation*.

### About the Authors' Credentials
These three are among the most distinguished decision scientists in the world. Raiffa, in particular, is legendary in the field — his 1968 book *Decision Analysis* helped establish the discipline. The PrOACT model is a practical distillation of their combined lifetime of work.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Key Takeaways
- Problem definition is the most critical step — a well-framed problem is half-solved
- Generating alternatives is the highest-leverage activity in the process
- The even-swap method is a powerful tool for making tradeoffs explicit
- Psychological traps are the main threat to good decision-making
- The PrOACT model is comprehensive but can feel heavy for small decisions
- Differentiating means from ends (objectives) is a crucial insight

### Strengths
- The most systematic, practical decision-making framework available
- Authored by the world's leading decision analysts
- Each chapter builds logically on the previous
- The psychological traps chapter is excellent

### Limitations
- Can feel overly analytical and academic
- The even-swap and scoring methods require practice
- Less emphasis on intuition and gut feelings
- Doesn't address emotional or social dimensions of decision-making
- Some examples feel dated (late 1990s business context)

### Key Concepts Explained
- The book distills the authors' academic work in decision analysis (Raiffa's *Decision Analysis*, Keeney & Raiffa's *Decisions with Multiple Objectives*) into a practical guide
- The PrOACT framework complements (and predates) the Heath brothers' WRAP framework
- The concept of "proactive decision-making" — choosing what decisions to make — is itself a powerful idea

### Notable References
- Kahneman & Tversky (heuristics and biases — the psychological traps chapter)
- Howard Raiffa's own earlier work (the foundation of decision analysis)
- The authors' academic papers on multi-attribute utility theory
