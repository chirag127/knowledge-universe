### Author Bios

**Chip Heath** is a professor at Stanford Graduate School of Business, teaching courses on strategy and organizations. He has helped over 450 startups hone their business strategy and messaging.

**Dan Heath** is a senior fellow at the Center for the Advancement of Social Entrepreneurship (CASE) at Duke University. He is also co-founder of Thinkwell, a textbook publishing company.

Together, they have written four New York Times bestselling books: *Made to Stick* (2007), *Switch* (2010), *Decisive* (2013), and *The Power of Moments* (2017). Their books have sold over two million copies worldwide and have been translated into 30+ languages.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Key Takeaways
- The WRAP framework is memorable and practical — applicable to both business and personal decisions
- The four villains resonate because everyone recognizes them in their own thinking
- Ooching is perhaps the most immediately useful technique — test before committing
- The 10/10/10 test is a simple but powerful question to cut through emotional noise

### Strengths
- Exceptionally well-written with compelling stories and examples
- The framework is easy to remember and apply
- Grounded in solid research without being academic or dry
- Includes practical "clinics" to practice the framework

### Limitations
- Some techniques feel intuitive once stated (though we don't naturally use them)
- The framework can feel time-consuming for minor decisions
- Less emphasis on group/organizational decision-making dynamics
- Could benefit from more discussion of when NOT to use the process

### Notable References
- Kahneman & Tversky (cognitive biases)
- Gary Klein (recognition-primed decision making)
- Nudge theory (Thaler & Sunstein)
- Gigerenzer (heuristics)
