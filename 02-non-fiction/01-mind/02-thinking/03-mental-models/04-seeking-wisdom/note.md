## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
The most comprehensive single volume on the psychology of human misjudgment outside of academic textbooks. Bevelin synthesizes Darwin, Munger, and decades of psychological research into a single, coherent framework. Considered essential reading alongside *Poor Charlie's Almanack*.

### Key Strengths
- The 28 psychological tendencies are the most complete catalog available outside Munger's original speech
- Dense with quotations from Munger, Buffett, Einstein, Feynman, Montaigne, and many others
- The checklists in Appendix D are genuinely useful for real-world decision-making
- Bridges evolution, psychology, and practical wisdom

### Key Limitations
- The quote-heavy style can be difficult to read straight through
- Little original research—primarily a synthesis of others' work
- Some sections feel like annotated bibliographies rather than flowing prose
- The 3rd edition is hard to find and expensive

### Best Read With
- *Poor Charlie's Almanack* (the companion volume)
- *The Great Mental Models* series (more accessible entry point)
- *Influence* by Robert Cialdini (for persuasion-specific biases)

### Reading Time
- Total: ~8-12 hours
- Best consumed in small doses as a reference work
