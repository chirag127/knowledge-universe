## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
Provides the cognitive science foundation for the mental models movement. Explains *why* we need mental models: because our individual understanding is radically shallow. Also explains why mental models alone aren't enough—we need community.

### Key Strengths
- The illusion of explanatory depth is a genuinely important discovery
- Excellent integration of cognitive science with real-world implications
- The education and politics chapters are particularly insightful
- Accessible to non-specialists

### Key Limitations
- The middle chapters (embodied cognition, technology) feel less connected to the core thesis
- Some redundancy across chapters
- The prescriptions (Chapters 10-12) are less compelling than the diagnosis (Chapters 1-4)
- Sometimes overstates the novelty (similar ideas in Vygotsky, Hutchins, etc.)

### Best Read With
- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Kahneman) for the System 1/2 framework
- *The Elephant in the Brain* (Simler/Hanson) for hidden motives
- *The Half-Life of Facts* (Arbesman) for how knowledge changes collectively
- *The Great Mental Models* for tools to compensate for the illusion

### Reading Time
- Total: ~5-7 hours
- Per chapter: ~25-35 minutes
