## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
An essential meta-model for the mental models library. Most mental models books implicitly assume that models are permanent truths. Arbesman's crucial insight is that knowledge changes, often predictably. This should inform how we hold and apply mental models.

### Key Strengths
- The half-life metaphor is brilliant and sticky
- Scientometrics is a fascinating and under-explored field
- Well-researched with excellent examples
- Moderate length; doesn't overstay its welcome

### Key Limitations
- More descriptive than prescriptive
- Less practical than most mental models books
- Some chapters feel disconnected from the central metaphor
- Does not provide specific guidance on how to track fact half-lives

### Best Read With
- *The Knowledge Illusion* (complementary perspective on knowledge limits)
- *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (deeper epistemology)
- *The Great Mental Models* (for the implicit assumption that models are durable)

### Reading Time
- Total: ~4-6 hours
- Per chapter: ~25-35 minutes
