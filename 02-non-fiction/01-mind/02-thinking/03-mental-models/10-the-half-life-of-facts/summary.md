## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Knowledge is not static—it changes over time, and often in measurable, predictable ways. Scientific facts, medical knowledge, historical understanding, and even mathematical truths have "half-lives" analogous to radioactive decay: a certain proportion become obsolete or are revised within a given time period. By understanding the scientific study of science itself (scientometrics), we can predict which facts are likely to change, how quickly, and what new knowledge will emerge. This has profound implications for how we should approach learning, decision-making, and managing our own ignorance in an age of accelerating knowledge growth.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### Pillar 1: The Changing Nature of Facts
- **Fact Half-Lives**: Different fields have different rates of knowledge decay (medicine: ~45 years; mathematics: much longer).
- **The Pace of Discovery**: Knowledge grows exponentially in many fields.
- **The Asymptote of Truth**: Some facts converge toward certainty (e.g., the speed of light), while others remain provisional.

### Pillar 2: The Laws of Knowledge Growth
- **Moore's Law of Everything**: Many knowledge domains grow exponentially (not just computing).
- **Spread of Facts**: How new knowledge diffuses through scientific communities and society.
- **Hidden Knowledge**: Knowledge that exists but hasn't been connected or synthesized yet.

### Pillar 3: The Measurement of Knowledge
- **Scientometrics**: The quantitative study of science itself.
- **Bibliometrics**: Measuring knowledge through publication and citation patterns.
- **The Physics of Knowledge**: Treating knowledge as a physical system with measurable properties.

### Pillar 4: Knowledge Dynamics
- **Fact Phase Transitions**: How facts change state from hypothesis to accepted truth.
- **The Discovery of Error**: How mistakes are discovered and corrected over time.
- **The Human Side of Facts**: Social, institutional, and psychological factors in knowledge change.

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

1. **Half-Life of Facts**: The time it takes for half of the known facts in a field to become obsolete or revised.
2. **Scientometrics**: The quantitative study of scientific research and knowledge.
3. **Bibliometrics**: Statistical analysis of publications, citations, and academic output.
4. **Exponential Knowledge Growth**: The doubling of known facts in a field at regular intervals.
5. **Consensus Curve**: The S-shaped curve of how scientific consensus forms around a fact.
6. **Fact Phase Transition**: The process by which a hypothesis becomes accepted as fact.
7. **Hidden Knowledge**: Undiscovered connections between existing pieces of knowledge.
8. **The Asymptote of Truth**: The tendency of measurements to converge on a true value over time.
9. **Stigler's Law of Eponymy**: No scientific discovery is named after its original discoverer.
10. **Galton's Problem**: The difficulty of separating independent discovery from cultural diffusion.
11. **Multiple Discovery**: The phenomenon of the same discovery being made independently by different people.
12. **The Red Queen and Knowledge**: Running to stay in place—knowledge maintenance requires constant effort.
13. **Knowledge Networks**: The interconnected structure of facts within and across disciplines.
14. **Error Decay**: The rate at which incorrect facts are identified and corrected.
15. **Data Mortality**: The rate at which digital data becomes inaccessible or unreadable.
