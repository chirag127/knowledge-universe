## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
A straightforward, no-frills introduction to 30 essential mental models. Less ambitious than *Super Thinking* (fewer models) but more focused. Useful as a first book on mental models before tackling deeper works.

### Key Strengths
- Clear, consistent format for each model
- Practical focus; each model has application steps
- The "Anti-Mental Models" chapter is a genuinely useful concept
- Accessible for complete beginners

### Key Limitations
- Self-published quality (some typos, formatting issues)
- Little original thinking; restates existing frameworks
- Overlaps significantly with *Super Thinking* and *Great Mental Models*
- The author's voice can be repetitive

### Best Read With
- *Super Thinking* (for broader coverage of models)
- *The Great Mental Models Vol. 1* (for deeper treatment of core models)
- *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (for the System 1/2 background)

### Reading Time
- Total: ~4-6 hours
- Per model: ~8-12 minutes each
