## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Exceptional thinking is not about intelligence or knowledge—it's about having the right mental tools and knowing when to apply them. This book provides 30 specific mental models organized around 5 themes: decision-making, clear perception, problem-solving, avoidance strategies, and timeless heuristics. Each model is a practical framework you can apply immediately to make better decisions, see more clearly, solve problems more effectively, and avoid common thinking traps. The key is not to master all 30 at once but to build a toolkit of go-to frameworks for different situations.

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

1. **Eisenhower Matrix**: Prioritize by urgency and importance; do important/non-urgent first.
2. **Second-Order Thinking**: Tracing the chain of consequences beyond the immediate.
3. **Satisficing**: Choosing an option that meets minimum criteria rather than seeking the perfect one.
4. **40-70% Rule**: Make decisions when you have 40-70% of the information you think you need.
5. **Regret Minimization**: The "Jeff Bezos" framework—minimize future regret.
6. **Bayesian Thinking**: Update prior beliefs with new evidence.
7. **Darwinian Objectivity**: Give special attention to disconfirming evidence.
8. **System 2 Thinking**: Kahneman's slow, deliberate, analytical mode of thought.
9. **SCAMPER**: A creative problem-solving technique through seven types of transformations.
10. **First Principles**: Breaking problems down to fundamental truths and rebuilding.
11. **Anti-Goals**: A list of things to avoid rather than pursue.
12. **Sturgeon's Law**: 90% of everything is mediocre—be selective.
13. **Parkinson's Law**: Work expands to fill the time available.
14. **Parkinson's Law of Triviality**: Organizations give disproportionate weight to trivial issues.
15. **Ishikawa (Fishbone) Diagram**: A tool for root cause analysis.
