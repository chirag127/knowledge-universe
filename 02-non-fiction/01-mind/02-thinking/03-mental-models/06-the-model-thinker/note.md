## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
The most rigorous formal treatment of the mental models concept. While Munger, Parrish, and Bevelin talk about collecting mental models as a conceptual practice, Page actually provides the mathematics. This is what the mental models movement looks like when taken seriously by an academic.

### Key Strengths
- The many-model paradigm is the formal justification for why we need diverse mental models
- REDCAPE framework provides a rigorous taxonomy for why we model
- The diversity prediction theorem alone is worth the price of the book
- Excellent reference for practitioners

### Key Limitations
- Requires mathematical comfort (algebra-level, but lots of it)
- Too dense for casual readers of the mental models genre
- Some chapters are too short to build deep competence in any one model class
- Less practical than Parrish or Bevelin for immediate application

### Best Read With
- *The Difference* (Page's earlier book on diversity)
- *The Great Mental Models Vol. 3* (less rigorous but more accessible)
- *Super Thinking* (broader catalog, less math)

### Reading Time
- Total: ~12-18 hours
- Best read chapter-by-chapter with time between to absorb
