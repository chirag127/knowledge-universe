## 📝 Personal Notes

### Why This Book Matters
The primary source document for the mental models movement. Every subsequent book on mental models—Parrish, Bevelin, Weinberg—traces its lineage to Munger's USC and Stanford talks. This is the closest thing to a holy text in the rationalist/decision-making community.

### Key Strengths
- Munger's voice is unique—witty, irreverent, and profoundly wise
- The Psychology of Human Misjudgment speech is arguably the most important 50 pages on cognitive bias ever written outside academia
- The latticework concept is genuinely transformative for how you approach problems
- The 2023 edition is beautifully produced

### Key Limitations
- Non-linear structure can frustrate readers seeking a systematic framework
- Some content is specific to investing and less applicable outside that domain
- Munger's biases sometimes show (he dismisses some valid academic work)
- Expensive, especially the collector-oriented editions

### Best Read With
- *Seeking Wisdom* (Bevelin) for systematic expansion of Munger's framework
- *The Great Mental Models Vol. 1* (Parrish) for modern, accessible reformulation
- *The Essays of Warren Buffett* (Cunningham) for the partner's perspective

### Reading Time
- Total: ~10-15 hours
- Best consumed as an ongoing reference rather than in one sitting
