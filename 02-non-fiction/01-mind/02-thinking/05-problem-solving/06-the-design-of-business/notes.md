## 📝 Personal Notes

### Key Concepts Summary

1. **Knowledge Funnel**: Mystery (unexplained) → Heuristic (rule of thumb) → Algorithm (predictable formula)
2. **Reliability**: Produces consistent results; measurable; eliminates judgment; scales well
3. **Validity**: Produces the right answer; may be inconsistent; requires judgment
4. **Abductive Logic**: "What might be true?" — the logic of design thinking and innovation
5. **Design Thinking**: The practice of balancing reliability and validity to move knowledge through the funnel

### Key Takeaways for Practice

- Diagnose your organization's reliability bias — where is validity being sacrificed for consistency?
- Actively push knowledge back up the funnel: re-engage with mysteries when you're too comfortable with algorithms
- Practice abductive logic: when data doesn't fit, ask "what might be true?" instead of dismissing it
- Create organizational space for both reliability (operations) and validity (innovation)
- As a leader, model the balance — be explicit about which mode you're in

### Gaps & Critiques

- More a strategic framework than a practical how-to guide
- The Procter & Gamble case is compelling but may not generalize to smaller organizations
- Limited discussion of implementation challenges and failure modes
- Abductive logic is introduced but not deeply operationalized
- Some of the examples (BlackBerry/RIM) aged poorly
- Does not address the role of digital transformation and data science
