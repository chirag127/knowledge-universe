## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Most people rely on intuition, habit, and subjective judgment when making decisions — and they make predictable errors as a result. Drawing on his CIA analytical training, Morgan Jones provides 14 systematic techniques that force objective, structured analysis. These tools — from problem restatement and pros-cons-and-fixes to causal flow diagramming, decision trees, probability trees, utility analysis, hypothesis testing, and devil's advocacy — are designed to combat cognitive biases and reveal hidden assumptions. Each technique can be learned and applied with nothing more than pencil and paper.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

**Pillar 1: Why We Go Astray (Part One)**
The human mind is subject to cognitive biases that distort judgment. Two introductory chapters explain the psychology of poor decision-making and set the stage for structured techniques to overcome these biases.

**Pillar 2: Problem Structuring Tools (Chapters 3-9)**
Techniques for clarifying and structuring problems: Problem Restatement, Pros-Cons-and-Fixes, Divergent/Convergent Thinking, Sorting/Chronologies/Timelines, Causal Flow Diagramming, The Matrix, and Scenario (Decision/Event) Trees.

**Pillar 3: Analytical & Evaluation Tools (Chapters 10-16)**
Techniques for rigorous analysis and evaluation: Weighted Ranking, Hypothesis Testing, Devil's Advocacy, Probability Tree, Utility Tree, Utility Matrix, and Advanced Utility Analysis.

**Pillar 4: Integration & Application (Chapter 17)**
How to combine and sequence tools based on the nature of the problem. Guidance on developing personal analytical discipline.
