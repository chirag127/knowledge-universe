### Author

**Twyla Tharp** — Born July 1, 1941, in Portland, Indiana. One of the most acclaimed choreographers of the 20th and 21st centuries. Founded Twyla Tharp Dance in 1965. Created dances for major companies worldwide (American Ballet Theatre, Paris Opera Ballet, Royal Ballet). Won Tony Award for *Movin' Out* (2003), two Emmy Awards, a MacArthur Fellowship. Also authored *The Creative Habit* and *Push Comes to Shove*.

**Mark Reiter** — Co-author and collaborator on multiple books.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Most Impactful Exercises

1. **Your Creative DNA** (Chapter 3) — Map the people, experiences, and works that shaped you creatively
2. **Memory Harvesting** (Chapter 4) — Write down 10 memories and use each as a creative prompt
3. **The Box Exercise** (Chapter 5) — Impose explicit constraints on a project and see how it focuses work
4. **Scratching Kit** (Chapter 6) — Create a physical or digital collection system for ideas and observations
5. **Spine Statement** (Chapter 8) — Articulate the core of your current project in one sentence
6. **Failure Resume** (Chapter 11) — List your failures and what each taught you

### Key Takeaways for Practice

- A daily ritual is non-negotiable — it makes starting automatic
- Know your creative DNA to play to your strengths
- Constraints focus creativity — don't fear the box
- Ideas come from active hunting, not passive waiting
- The spine of a project is its single-sentence essence
- Skill is the foundation of execution — practice deliberately
- Ruts are inevitable; have strategies ready to break them
- Failure is data, not verdict

### Gaps & Critiques

- Heavily grounded in performing arts — some metaphors may not translate perfectly to other domains
- Exercises require significant time commitment
- Less structured than typical business/self-help books
- Some concepts are introduced but not deeply explored (e.g., the neuroscience of habit)
- The privileges and resources of Tharp's career are not fully acknowledged as advantages
