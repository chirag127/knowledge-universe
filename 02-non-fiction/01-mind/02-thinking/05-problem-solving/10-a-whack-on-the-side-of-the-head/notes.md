### Author

**Roger von Oech** — Creativity consultant, speaker, and author. Founder of Creative Think, a consulting firm that helps organizations develop creative thinking. Author of *A Kick in the Seat of the Pants* and *Expect the Unexpected*. His creative thinking techniques have been used by companies worldwide.

### Editions Note

The original 1983 edition had 10 chapters (the 10 mental locks). The 2008 "Special Edition" added new material. The 2024 "Definitive Edition" is significantly expanded (352 pages) with new stories and exercises.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Most Powerful Whacks

1. **The Second Right Answer** (Ch 1) — Always ask "what's another answer?" after finding a good one
2. **The Foolish Idea Exercise** (Ch 8) — Generate the most foolish idea you can, then ask what makes it work
3. **Play as Strategy** (Ch 5) — The best ideas often emerge when you're not trying to be productive
4. **Error as Discovery** (Ch 9) — Post-it Notes, penicillin, microwave — all from "mistakes"
5. **The Self-Fulfilling Prophecy** (Ch 10) — "I'm not creative" is the lock that keeps all other locks in place

### Key Takeaways for Practice

- After finding a good answer, ask: "What's the second right answer?"
- Question at least one rule per week — "What if we did the opposite?"
- Let yourself be impractical first — practicality comes later
- Schedule play time — it's not a luxury, it's essential
- Read outside your field regularly — cross-pollination is a creativity superpower
- When you make a mistake, ask: "What can I learn from this?"
- Give yourself permission to be foolish — the best ideas often start there

### Gaps & Critiques

- Some examples feel dated (e.g., 1980s technology references)
- The structure is more inspirational than systematic
- Less rigorous than other creativity books (no research citations)
- Some mental locks overlap significantly
- Limited guidance on organizational implementation
- The "whack" metaphor may feel gimmicky to some readers
