## 📝 Personal Notes

### Most Impactful Case Studies

1. **Sydney Airport Capacity** (Chapter 1) — Demonstrates how logic trees break down a complex infrastructure question
2. **Solar Panel Investment** (Chapter 1) — Shows NPV and breakeven analysis in a personal finance context
3. **Career Choice** (Chapter 8) — Applies the framework to life's most important decisions
4. **Pacific Salmon Strategy** (Chapter 8) — Portfolio management for long-term environmental strategy
5. **Obesity as Wicked Problem** (Chapter 9) — Illustrates how traditional problem solving fails on socially complex issues

### Key Takeaways for Practice

- Always start with problem definition — resist the urge to jump to solutions
- The quality of your logic tree determines the quality of your answer
- Generate a "one-day answer" before building the workplan
- Use heuristics first; escalate to advanced tools only when needed
- Spend 30% of your time on analysis, 70% on synthesis and communication
- The pyramid principle is non-negotiable for professional communication

### Gaps & Critiques

- Heavy consulting/McKinsey orientation may feel foreign to non-business readers
- Some case studies are simplified compared to real-world complexity
- Chapter 6's "Big Guns" may intimidate readers without quantitative backgrounds
- Less depth on implementation and change management after the solution is found
- Could benefit from more on cognitive biases and behavioral obstacles
