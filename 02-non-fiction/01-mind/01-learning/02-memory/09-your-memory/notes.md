## 📝 Personal Notes

### Introduction & Chapter 1
- "Thinking person's memory book"—bridges academic and popular
- 10 myths: especially important are #4 ("stuck with bad memory") and #8 ("never forgets")
- Metamemory: what you believe about memory affects how you use it

### Chapter 2: What Is Memory?
- Three Rs: Recording (encoding), Retaining (storage), Retrieving (recall)
- STM: ~7 items, temporary
- LTM: unlimited, permanent (but retrievability varies)
- Tip-of-the-tongue = retrieval failure, not storage loss

### Chapter 3: How Does It Work?
- Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve: fastest drop in first hour
- Interference (proactive and retroactive)
- Picture superiority: images > words for memorability
- Sleep learning: largely ineffective

### Chapter 4: Basic Principles (1–5)
1. Meaningfulness: impose meaning on material
2. Organization: categorize, structure
3. Association: connect to existing knowledge
4. Visualization: create mental images
5. Attention: focus is prerequisite

### Chapter 5: More Basic Principles (6–10)
6. Repetition: spaced > massed
7. Relaxation: anxiety blocks encoding
8. Context: match encoding/retrieval environments
9. Interest: genuine engagement
10. Feedback: check your knowledge

### Chapter 6: Study Skills
- Distributed practice: multiple sessions > one marathon
- Chunking: break into manageable pieces
- Recitation: say it aloud
- SQ3R: Survey → Question → Read → Recite → Review
- Reduce interference: separate similar subjects

### Chapter 7: Intro to Mnemonics
- First-letter mnemonics: acronyms (NASA), acrostics (My Very Educated Mother...)
- Keyword Method for vocabulary
- Principles: vividness, interaction, absurdity

### Chapter 8: Legitimacy
- Real limits: don't aid comprehension, require effort, can decay
- Pseudo-limits: not "just tricks," not "unnatural"
- Mnemonics aid retention, not understanding

### Chapter 9: Link & Story
- Link: A→B→C chain of interacting images
- Story: coherent narrative wrapping all items
- Best for short ordered lists (5-20 items)

### Chapter 10: Loci
- Oldest system (Simonides, ~500 BCE)
- Familiar building → specific locations → place images
- Best for long ordered lists, speeches, presentations
- Research shows significant improvement

### Chapter 11: Peg
- Higbee's phonetic pegs: 1=tie, 2=Noah, 3=ma, 4=ray, 5=law, 6=jaw, 7=key, 8=foe, 9=pea, 10=toe
- Allows instant access by position number
- Combine with Loci for power

### Chapter 12: Phonetic System
- Major System mapping: 1=t/d, 2=n, 3=m, 4=r, 5=l, 6=sh/j/ch, 7=k/g, 8=f/v, 9=p/b, 0=s/z
- Appendix provides keywords for 00-99
- Best for numbers, dates, formulas
- Requires the most practice

### Chapter 13: Names and Faces
1. Get the name
2. Repeat it
3. Observe facial features
4. Choose distinctive feature
5. Convert name to image
6. Link feature to name image
7. Review

### Chapter 14: Absentmindedness & Education
- Prospective memory: external aids + visual reminders + habit
- Retrospective memory: review + mnemonic systems
- Education: vocabulary, languages, formulas, facts
- Understanding + mnemonics = powerful combination
