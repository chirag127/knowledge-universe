## 📝 Personal Notes

### Introduction: What If Genius Isn't Born—It's Built?
- "Genius" = stack of trainable cognitive skills
- Follow-up to *Remember It!* — expands from memory to full cognitive toolkit
- Each section is self-contained; can be read independently

### Section 1: Memory Mastery
- Advanced SEE-LINK-GO
- PAO system refinement for speed
- Card memorization competition techniques
- Multi-palace management
- Integration of memory into other domains

### Section 2: Speed Reading
- Subvocalization elimination
- Peripheral vision expansion
- Meta-guiding (finger/pen as pacer)
- Text previewing and structure recognition
- Comprehension vs. speed tradeoff

### Section 3: Focus and Concentration
- Flow state: clear goals, immediate feedback, challenge-skill balance
- Deep work: distraction-free blocks of concentrated work
- Pomodoro Technique: 25-min focus → 5-min break
- Attention residue: the cost of task switching
- Building focus through practice

### Section 4: Learning Mastery
- Feynman Technique: explain simply → identify gaps → re-learn
- Spaced repetition: increasing review intervals
- Interleaving: mix topics during practice
- Deliberate practice: targeted, feedback-rich, challenging
- Active recall: test yourself, don't re-read

### Section 5: Mental Calculation
- Squaring numbers ending in 5: (n × n+1) + 25
- Multiplication shortcuts: 11×, 15%, etc.
- Estimation and rounding for quick approximations
- Cross-multiplication for 2-digit × 2-digit
- Percentage shortcuts: 10% → 5% → 1%

### Section 6: Problem-Solving and Creativity
- Lateral thinking: indirect, creative approaches
- SCAMPER: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse
- Problem decomposition: break into sub-problems
- Divergent (idea generation) vs. convergent (idea selection) thinking
- Incubation: step away for creative breakthroughs

### Section 7: Strategic Thinking
- N+1 thinking: model outcomes 1, 2, 3 moves ahead
- Pattern recognition: identify recurring structures
- Risk assessment: probability × impact
- Positional thinking: improve your overall position, not just the immediate move
- Mental models from chess, military strategy, and business

### Section 8: Social Skills Mastery
- Room reading: body language, power dynamics, mood
- Advanced name recall: systematic approach for groups
- Active listening: paraphrase, ask follow-ups, maintain eye contact
- Conversation threading: build on what was said
- Thinking on your feet: pause, structure, respond

### Section 9: Beyond Genius
- Integration across all 8 domains
- Cognitive lifestyle: sleep, exercise, nutrition
- Curiosity as fuel for lifelong learning
- The 30-Day Genius Challenge
- Genius is a practice, not a destination
