## 📝 Personal Notes

### Chapter 1: Introduction
- Mnemonics aid memory for details, not understanding
- Most underused formal memory aid among students
- Research proves effectiveness, but students don't believe it
- Key: understanding *why* a technique works increases use

### Chapter 2: First Letter Mnemonics
- Acronyms: NASA, HOMES, SCUBA
- Acrostics: "My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nine Pizzas"
- Best for: short arbitrary lists with stable order
- Limitation: can't recall the acronym itself

### Chapter 3: Rhythm & Rhyme
- Phonological loop: auditory memory is sticky
- "Thirty days hath September"
- "I before E except after C"
- "In 1492, Columbus sailed the ocean blue"
- Best for: facts that can be put in poetic form

### Chapter 4: The Keyword Method
- Step 1: Find keyword (sound-alike familiar word)
- Step 2: Create image linking keyword to meaning
- Spanish "pato" (duck) → "pot" → duck in pot
- Most researched vocabulary technique
- Vastly superior to rote repetition

### Chapter 5: Extensions of Keyword Method
- Face-name: feature + name keyword
- Medical terminology: "gluteus" → "glue"
- Works for any new term that can be sound-alike matched

### Chapter 6: The Story Method
- Narrative wrapping: items become story elements
- Natural and intuitive
- Best for 5-20 items
- Breaks down for very long lists

### Chapter 7: The Place Method
- Method of Loci / Memory Palace / Journey Method
- Oldest known mnemonic (Greek, ~500 BCE)
- Spatial memory is evolutionarily ancient and powerful
- Best for: long ordered sequences, speeches

### Chapter 8: The Pegword Method
- 1=bun, 2=shoe, 3=tree, 4=door, 5=hive
- 6=sticks, 7=heaven, 8=gate, 9=vine, 10=hen
- Instant access by position number
- Combines with Place Method for power

### Chapter 9: The Link Method
- Pure visual chaining: A → B → C
- No narrative (vs. Story Method)
- Faster but less durable than Story Method
- Best for short sequences where speed matters

### Chapter 10: Coding Mnemonics
- Major System: 1=t/d, 2=n, 3=m, 4=r, 5=l
- 6=sh/j/ch, 7=k/g, 8=f/v, 9=p/b, 0=s/z
- Numbers → words → images
- Most difficult but necessary for numbers, formulas

### Chapter 11: Mastering Mnemonics
- Strategy selection: match technique to task
- Integration: combine techniques for complex material
- Habit change: the real challenge
- Deliberate practice: use it or lose it

### Chapter 12: Case Study
- Geological Time Scale
- Integrates: first-letter (periods), keyword (hard names), place (chronology), pegword (numbers), coding (dates)
- Step-by-step walkthrough
- Demonstrates real-world application
