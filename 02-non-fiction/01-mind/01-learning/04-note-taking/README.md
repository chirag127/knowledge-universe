# Note-Taking & Knowledge Management

Welcome to the **Note-Taking & Knowledge Management** section of your Library of Alexandria. This branch covers structural systems for capturing ideas, Personal Knowledge Management (PKM), mind mapping, and the Zettelkasten method to build a connected, long-term memory engine.

---

## 🧠 Core Principles of Note-Taking & Knowledge Management

1. **Atomicity**: Keeping notes focused on a single, self-contained concept (atomic notes) to maximize their modularity and reusability.
2. **Connectivity**: Interlinking related notes to build a web of ideas, allowing unexpected connections to surface over time (e.g., Zettelkasten).
3. **Structured Capturing**: Creating clear pathways to capture (Capture), categorize (Organize), filter (Distill), and utilize (Express) notes.
4. **Active Reflection**: Treating note-taking as a cognitive thinking partner rather than a passive storage vault.
