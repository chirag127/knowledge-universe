## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

A Zettelkasten is not primarily a note-taking system — it is a *writing* system. By structuring the flow from fleeting notes through reference notes to main notes, and then leveraging those notes through creative logs, structure notes, and daily journals, you transform idea capture into consistent written output across all scales — from social media posts to books — without ever facing a blank page.

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

1. **Reference Note** — Doto's term for literature note; condensed source summary with citation.
2. **Main Note** — Doto's term for permanent note; one atomic idea per note.
3. **Structure Note** — A note that links to other notes to create thematic clusters or outlines.
4. **Hub Note** — Entry-point note for a topic; links to all relevant main notes.
5. **Fleeting Note** — Temporary capture in inbox; must be processed or discarded.
6. **Creative Log** — Separate log for tracking writing projects, goals, and progress.
7. **Writing Ladder** — The spectrum from small (social media) to large (books) outputs.
8. **Trains of Thought** — Sequences of linked notes that develop a single argument.
9. **Dialectical Linking** — Connecting contradictory ideas to surface tension and depth.
10. **Folgezettel** — Sequential numbering for physical note ordering; Doto adapts for digital.
11. **Interstitial Journaling** — Brief notes between tasks to capture context switches.
12. **Sleeping Folder** — Archive for promising but currently irrelevant notes.
