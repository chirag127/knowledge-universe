## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- **Shows actual notes**: One of the few Zettelkasten books that includes photographs/screenshots of real notes at various stages
- **Checklists**: Every chapter ends with a concrete action list
- **Writing-focused**: Unique emphasis on output rather than just input
- **Creative Log**: A genuinely novel contribution to the genre
- **Practical naming**: Renames Luhmann's terminology into clearer English (Reference Notes, Main Notes)
- **Accessible tone**: Direct, no-nonsense, avoids academic jargon

### Weaknesses / Criticisms
- **Very new** (2024): Fewer reviews and less community validation than older books
- **Light on research**: Practice-based rather than evidence-based
- **Self-published**: Less editorial rigor than traditional publishers
- **Narrow scope**: Focused exclusively on Zettelkasten for writing; less useful for other note-taking goals
- **Terminology differences**: Renaming established Zettelkasten terms may confuse readers who've read Ahrens

### Key Quotes
- "What is your Zettelkasten for? Most other works focus on getting stuff into a Zettelkasten, and often don't bother to ask to what purpose."
- "Writer's block sounds hard to overcome, but who ever has conversation block?"
- "Writing is not the end of note-making — it is a continuation of it by other means."

### Reviews Summary
- *Interdependent Thoughts* (Ton Zijlstra): "Doto weaves his showing and telling together, making it a much more useful practice-oriented text."
- *WebSeitz*: "Another 'Zettelkasten primer' won't be needed for some time, since this one is direct, concise, thorough and strongly practical."
- *Lifelong Learners* (Dan Allosso): Praises the actionable nature and clear note examples.
