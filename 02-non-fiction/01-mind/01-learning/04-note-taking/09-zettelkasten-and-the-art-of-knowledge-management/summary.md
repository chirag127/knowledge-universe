## 💡 The Big Idea

Knowledge does not live in isolation — it is fundamentally connected to the people, places, and events of our lives. Most PKM teaching treats knowledge as standalone, missing the rich context of when and how we learned it. By combining Zettelkasten note-taking with a **Graph Journal** (a daily journal where entries are linked to recurring subjects), you create a unified knowledge graph that maps not just what you know, but how your life and learning intertwine. This personal knowledge ecosystem enables better insight, clearer goals, and more meaningful action.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### 1. PKM as Learning Management + Personal Information Management
Binny's foundational equation: PKM = Learning Management (what you learn) + Personal Information Management (what happens to you). Most PKM books focus only on the first half; Binny insists on both.

### 2. Zettelkasten as the Core Learning Method
The classic Zettelkasten workflow: fleeting notes → literature notes → permanent notes. Atomic (one idea per note), written in your own words, interlinked. The engine of understanding and recall.

### 3. The Graph Journal
Binny's original contribution: a daily journal where each entry links to recurring subjects (people, projects, places, topics). This creates a graph of your life events, not just your ideas, enabling you to see patterns across time and domains.

### 4. PKM Flywheel (Capture → Manage → Use)
Three habits form a virtuous cycle: capture information when encountered, regularly manage your vault (review, link, prune), and actively use it for projects, content creation, and problem-solving.

### 5. Obsidian as the Recommended Tool
The book is Obsidian-centric but principles apply to any tool. Covers graph view, markdown, metadata, YAML, and taxonomy. The tool should serve the methodology, not vice versa.

### 6. AI as a PKM Assistant (Not Replacement)
Large language models (ChatGPT, etc.) can help collect, summarize, and connect information — but the thinking must remain human. AI is a tool in the PKM toolkit, not a substitute for understanding.

### 7. Note-Taking System Flexibility
While Zettelkasten is the primary method, Binny also covers Progressive Summarization (Tiago Forte), the Feynman Technique, and other systems. The key requirements: atomic notes, own words, interlinked.

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

1. **PKM (Personal Knowledge Management)**: Systematic process of managing and processing information; = Learning Management + Personal Information Management.
2. **Zettelkasten**: German for "slip box"; a note-taking method using atomic, linked notes to build a knowledge network.
3. **Atomic Note**: A note containing exactly one idea, written in your own words, complete enough to stand alone.
4. **Fleeting Note**: Temporary, quick capture note; discarded after processing into literature/permanent notes.
5. **Literature Note**: A summary of a single source (book, article, video), in your own words.
6. **Permanent Note**: The core Zettelkasten note type; one atomic idea, interlinked, permanently stored.
7. **Graph Journal**: Binny's method of daily journaling where entries link to recurring subjects, creating a life-knowledge graph.
8. **Knowledge Vault**: The central repository of all notes; the single source of truth for your PKM system.
9. **Bidirectional Link**: A connection between notes that can be traversed in either direction ([[wikilink]]).
10. **PKM Flywheel**: The capture → manage → use habit loop that drives PKM growth.
11. **Graph View**: Visual representation of note connections as a network diagram.
12. **Metadata**: Structured data (tags, dates, links) that makes notes searchable and filterable.
13. **YAML Frontmatter**: Metadata block at the top of a markdown note for machine-readable information.
14. **Taxonomy**: A hierarchical classification scheme for organizing notes by category.
15. **Spaced Repetition**: Reviewing notes at increasing intervals to cement long-term memory.
16. **Progressive Summarization**: Tiago Forte's method of layering summaries on notes (bold → highlight → executive summary).
17. **Feynman Technique**: Learning by teaching a concept in the simplest possible language.
18. **Transferable Ideas**: Concepts, models, and frameworks that apply across domains.
19. **Knowledge Worker**: Anyone whose primary work involves processing and creating information.
20. **Mere-Exposure Effect**: The cognitive bias where re-reading creates an illusion of understanding without true comprehension.
