## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

The PARA method simplifies digital organization to four categories — Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives — that cover everything in your life. By organizing information by *actionability* (what you're doing) rather than by *topic* (what things are about), you can implement the system in 60 seconds and maintain it with minimal effort, freeing mental energy for what matters.

## 🔑 Thematic Pillars & Core Frameworks

### 1. The Four PARA Categories
| Category | Definition | Examples | Actionability |
|----------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **P**rojects | Short-term outcomes with deadlines | "Website redesign," "Tax filing" | Highest |
| **A**reas | Long-term responsibilities | "Health," "Finances," "Team management" | Ongoing |
| **R**esources | Topics of potential interest | "UX design articles," "Travel inspiration" | Reference |
| **A**rchives | Inactive items from above | Completed projects, past areas | Lowest |

### 2. The Key Principle
Organize information by your **projects and goals**, not by the information's topic. Actionability > taxonomy.

### 3. The Sixty-Second Setup
1. Create four top-level folders: Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives
2. Move existing files into the appropriate category
3. Done. (Yes, really.)

### 4. The Three Core Habits
1. **Project List Review** — Weekly: check all projects are current
2. **Weekly Review** — Sort inbox, update statuses
3. **Monthly Archive** — Move inactive items to Archives

### 5. Best Practice Tips
- Archive freely (don't overthink what goes where)
- Use folders, not tags (simpler)
- Distinguish Projects from Areas (a project has an end date)
- Don't over-nest (max 2-3 levels deep)
- Add an Inbox folder for incoming items

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

1. **PARA** — Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives — the four-category system.
2. **Project** — A specific, short-term outcome with a deadline or goal. Has an end date.
3. **Area** — An ongoing sphere of responsibility without a fixed end date. Needs maintenance.
4. **Resource** — A topic or interest that may become useful. Reference material.
5. **Archive** — Inactive items from other categories. Safety net, not trash.
6. **Actionability** — The principle of organizing by what you need to DO, not what things ARE.
7. **Inbox** — A temporary holding space for new items before sorting into PARA.
8. **Project List** — A master list of all active projects; the primary navigation tool.
9. **Organize as Little as Possible** — The core efficiency philosophy.
10. **Just-in-Time Organization** — Organize only when information becomes actionable.
11. **Platform Agnostic** — PARA works in any app (files, notes, cloud storage, etc.).
12. **Cognitive Load Reduction** — The benefit of having a unified organizational system.
