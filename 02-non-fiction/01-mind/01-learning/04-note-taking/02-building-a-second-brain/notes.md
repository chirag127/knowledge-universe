## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- Most accessible introduction to personal knowledge management for beginners
- CODE and PARA are memorable acronyms that stick
- Progressive Summarization is a genuinely useful technique
- Rich with real-world examples from course graduates
- Strong emphasis on *actionability* over archiving

### Weaknesses / Criticisms
- **Overly verbose**: Many reviews note the book could be 1/3 the length
- **Light on research**: Uses extended mind theory superficially; little original evidence
- **Rehashed content**: Blog posts and course materials repackaged as a book
- **Generic advice**: Some critics note PARA is common sense rebranded
- **No mention of predecessors**: Ignores Zettelkasten, commonplace book tradition
- **Tech-focused**: Those wanting paper-based systems will find little here

### Key Quotes
- "We want to organize as little as possible, as late as possible, and only as much as absolutely needed."
- "A Second Brain is a digital commonplace — a personal knowledge management system."
- "Have a place for everything — then you have to think about it only once."

### Common Criticisms from Reviews
1. "Could have been a blog post" — many repeat this sentiment
2. "All tell, no show" — lacks concrete examples of notes themselves
3. "Not for academics" — focused on creative/business use, not research
4. "Reads like a sales funnel" — promotes paid course throughout

### See Also
- **Author's Other Works**: *The PARA Method* (2023), *Life in Perspective*
- **Predecessor**: *Getting Things Done* by David Allen
- **Complement**: *How to Take Smart Notes* by Sönke Ahrens (deeper theory)
- **Course**: buildingasecondbrain.com/course
- **Tool**: Evernote, Notion, OneNote (Forte uses these)
