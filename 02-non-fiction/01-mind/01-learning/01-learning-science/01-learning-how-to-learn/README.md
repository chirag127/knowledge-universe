# Learning How to Learn: How to Succeed in School Without Spending All Your Time Studying

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Barbara Oakley, Terrence Sejnowski, Alistair McConville
* **Published**: 2018
* **Description**: A highly practical, kid- and teen-friendly guide adapting the core concepts of cognitive psychology and neuroscience to explain brain links, attentional focus, active study tricks, and test-taking strategies.

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are a student, parent, or teacher who is new to the science of learning and wants the full visual and narrative experience — the book uses cartoons, metaphors, and exercises that summaries cannot replicate. The repetition and interactive pauses throughout the book are intentional pedagogy: they make the techniques stick. If your goal is not just to know these ideas but to actually change your study habits, the full book is the better investment.

**Use the summaries instead if** you are already familiar with concepts like retrieval practice, spaced repetition, and the Pomodoro Technique from *Make It Stick* or *A Mind for Numbers*. The core frameworks are nearly identical across all three Oakley books. Readers who have completed any of her other works will find this book largely redundant at the concept level — its unique value is in its accessible format for younger learners.

> **Verdict**: *New to learning science → read the book. Already know Oakley's concepts from another source → the summaries here are sufficient.*
