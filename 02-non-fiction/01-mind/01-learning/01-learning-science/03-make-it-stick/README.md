# Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Peter C. Brown, Henry L. Roediger III, Mark A. McDaniel
* **Published**: 2014
* **Edition**: 1st (Harvard University Press)
* **Genre**: Learning Science / Cognitive Psychology / Education
* **Description**: A rigorous, research-backed guide that dismantles the most widely used study myths — rereading, highlighting, and cramming — and replaces them with evidence-based techniques like retrieval practice, spaced repetition, and interleaving that produce durable, transferable mastery.
* **Companion Books**: *A Mind for Numbers* (Barbara Oakley), *Powerful Teaching* (Agarwal & Bain), *Learning How to Learn* (Oakley, Sejnowski)

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are a student, educator, coach, or corporate trainer who wants both the scientific evidence *and* actionable techniques. The book's strength is its narrative framing — each chapter opens with a real case study (a pilot, a doctor, a football coach) that makes the abstract research concrete and memorable. The repetitive revisiting of core concepts across chapters is deliberate — the authors practice what they preach. Reading sequentially gives you the full pedagogical benefit.

**Use the summaries instead if** you are already applying spaced repetition and retrieval practice in your daily study and simply want a reference for concepts like interleaving, generation, and calibration. The research findings themselves (the testing effect, the spacing effect, the Kornell & Bjork interleaving study) are well-documented in the summaries here. Experienced learners familiar with cognitive psychology will gain little new from the case study narratives.

> **Verdict**: *Teaching others, building new study habits from scratch, or need research citations → read the full book. Already using retrieval and spacing daily → the summaries here are sufficient.*
