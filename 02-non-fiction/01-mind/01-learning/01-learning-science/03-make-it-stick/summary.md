# Book Summary: Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning

## Overview
Traditional study methods like rereading, highlighting, and cramming create a false sense of mastery (the illusion of competence) because they require minimal cognitive effort. Durable, long-term learning requires effortful retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving, and generation. When learning feels difficult, the brain is actively rebuilding and strengthening neural connections.

---

### Core Learning Takeaways
* **Pillar 1**: Explains how cognitive psychology's empirical findings prove that memory retrieval, spaced practice, and interleaving are crucial for durable learning, debunking common study myths like rereading and highlighting.
* **Pillar 2**: Real-world application of memory, consolidation, and practice.
* **Pillar 3**: Overcoming study illusions through active, effortful retrieval.
