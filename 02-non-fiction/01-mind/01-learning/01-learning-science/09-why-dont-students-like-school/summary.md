# Book Summary: Why Don't Students Like School?

## Overview
The human brain is not naturally optimized for slow, effortful thinking; it relies heavily on memory to guide action. We enjoy cognitive challenges but only when they are solvable. To think critically, factual knowledge must be stored in long-term memory first. Working memory limits can be bypassed by automating basic skills through practice.

---

### Core Learning Takeaways
* **Pillar 1**: An expert cognitive psychologist applies nine basic brain principles to explain how memory, attention, and practice work, detailing why our brains avoid effortful thought unless challenged at the proper level.
* **Pillar 2**: Real-world application of memory, consolidation, and practice.
* **Pillar 3**: Overcoming study illusions through active, effortful retrieval.
