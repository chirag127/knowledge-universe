## 📝 Personal Notes

### Foreword — Terrence J. Sejnowski
*Sejnowski provides a neuroscientist's validation of Oakley's approach. He
emphasizes that the brain's learning mechanisms evolved for survival, not
abstract reasoning — which is why math feels unnatural.*

### Preface — Jeffrey D. Karpicke
*Karpicke frames the book as a bridge between cognitive science labs and
classroom practice. His own research on retrieval practice is central to
the book's evidence base.*
