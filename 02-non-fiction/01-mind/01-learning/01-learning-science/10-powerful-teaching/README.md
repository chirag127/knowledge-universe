# Powerful Teaching: Unleash the Science of Learning

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Pooja K. Agarwal, Patrice M. Bain
* **Published**: 2019
* **Edition**: 1st (Jossey-Bass)
* **Genre**: Learning Science / Educational Practice / Classroom Methods
* **Description**: A highly practical guide translating cognitive science research into classroom instruction. The book introduces four "Power Tools" — retrieval practice, spacing, interleaving, and feedback-driven metacognition — to improve student retention and performance with zero additional prep or grading time.
* **Companion Books**: *Make It Stick* (Brown et al.), *Small Teaching* (Lang), *Understanding How We Learn* (Weinstein & Sumeracki)

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are a classroom teacher, school administrator, or professional trainer looking for exact, day-to-day templates for evidence-based instruction. The book is written as a collaboration between a cognitive scientist (Agarwal) and a veteran middle school teacher (Bain), providing a unique bridge between scientific theory and real-world classroom mechanics. It features extensive scripts of classroom dialogues, detailed examples of low-stakes quizzes, instructions on how to set up "Metacognition Sheets," and guides on how to discuss learning science directly with parents and students.

**Use the summaries instead if** you are a self-directed learner, student, or corporate professional looking to apply these principles to your own studying. While the cognitive models are highly valuable, the vast majority of the book is structured around classroom management, lesson planning, school administrative challenges, and how to communicate with parents. If you want a clean, direct, and actionable explanation of the four core cognitive "Power Tools" to optimize your own memory, these summaries will give you the complete blueprint without the school-teaching scenarios and scripts.

> **Verdict**: *Read the full book if you are a teacher or school leader looking for concrete templates and scripts to integrate learning science into a school system. Use the summaries if you want a direct guide to apply the four "Power Tools" to your own personal learning.*
