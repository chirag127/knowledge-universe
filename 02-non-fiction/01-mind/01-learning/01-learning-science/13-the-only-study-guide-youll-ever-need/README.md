# The Only Study Guide You'll Ever Need

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Jade Bowler
* **Published**: 2021
* **Edition**: 1st (Blink Publishing)
* **Genre**: Learning Science / Study Skills / Student Wellbeing
* **Description**: A comprehensive study guide that combines cognitive science with student mental health and wellbeing. The book introduces the "SAAD" framework (Spaced repetition, Active recall, Associations, Desirable difficulty), details practical revision hacks like "blurting," and addresses perfectionism, procrastination, and exam anxiety.
* **Companion Books**: *Make It Stick* (Brown et al.), *How We Learn* (Carey), *A Mind for Numbers* (Oakley)

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are a high school or university student struggling with exam stress, burnout, and finding the motivation to study. Written by popular study YouTuber Jade Bowler ("UnJaded Jade"), the book uses a highly supportive, conversational, and peer-to-peer tone. It features custom habit trackers, colorful schedules, journaling prompts, and direct advice on how to handle the emotional pressure of academic environments, deal with imposter syndrome, and construct a balanced lifestyle that incorporates both study and self-care.

**Use the summaries instead if** you are an adult professional or self-directed learner who already has strong emotional self-regulation and simply wants to learn the specific cognitive study techniques. The core academic techniques (spaced repetition, active recall, interleaving, and mind palaces) are standard learning science models, and the specific "blurting" method is easily explained in a few sentences. If you do not need advice on high school anxiety, exam day protocols, parental pressure, or mental health journaling, these summaries will isolate the core learning methods for your immediate use.

> **Verdict**: *Read the full book if you are a student looking for a friendly, holistic guide that balances scientific study techniques with emotional wellbeing and anxiety management. Use the summaries if you want a direct, fluff-free catalog of the study hacks and the SAAD framework.*
