# Book Summary: How We Learn: The Surprising Truth About When, Where, and Why It Happens

## Overview
Forgetting is not an error; it is a critical cognitive filter that makes the remaining memories searchable. According to storage-retrieval strength theory, making retrieval difficult increases long-term storage strength. Varying your study environment, using spaced review, and taking breaks to allow incubation and sleep are the key to organic memory consolidation.

---

### Core Learning Takeaways
* **Pillar 1**: A popular science exploration of the brain's organic memory filters, detailing how context variation, forgetting, testing, percolation, and sleep drive long-term retention.
* **Pillar 2**: Real-world application of memory, consolidation, and practice.
* **Pillar 3**: Overcoming study illusions through active, effortful retrieval.
