# Understanding How We Learn: A Visual Guide

## Metadata
* **Authors**: Yana Weinstein, Megan Sumeracki (with Oliver Caviglioli)
* **Published**: 2018
* **Edition**: 1st (Routledge)
* **Genre**: Learning Science / Cognitive Psychology / Visual Learning
* **Description**: A highly visual translation of cognitive science into everyday learning practices. The book explores the fundamental processes of perception, attention, and memory, systematically refutes common educational myths, and outlines six key learning strategies: spaced practice, retrieval practice, interleaving, elaboration, concrete examples, and dual coding.
* **Companion Books**: *Make It Stick* (Brown et al.), *Small Teaching* (Lang), *Powerful Teaching* (Agarwal & Bain)

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are a highly visual learner, designer, parent, or teacher looking for an incredibly accessible, illustrated introduction to cognitive psychology. The book features the dual-coding layouts of designer Oliver Caviglioli, turning abstract scientific models into beautiful, scannable infographics, flowcharts, and diagrams. It contains specific, divided "helpsheets" and checklists for teachers, students, and parents, making it a perfect quick-reference guide to print out and keep on a classroom wall or home study desk.

**Use the summaries instead if** you prefer dense, detailed academic explanations over high-level visual summaries. The book is designed to be brief, clear, and simple; it purposefully avoids deep neurological jargon, statistical meta-analyses, and dense literature reviews to remain digestible. If you have already read comprehensive books like *Make It Stick* and want to skip the high-level explanations and visual diagrams to get a direct, textual summary of the six cognitive strategies and their implementation tips, these summaries are sufficient.

> **Verdict**: *Read the full book if you want a highly visual, illustrated, and scannable introduction to learning science with clear infographics. Use the summaries if you want a direct, text-based catalog of the six strategies and implementation checklists.*
