# Learning Science

Welcome to the **Learning Science** section of your Library of Alexandria. Learning science is the interdisciplinary field that studies how people learn, drawing from cognitive psychology, neuroscience, educational psychology, and computer science.

Rather than relying on intuition or traditional study habits (which are often ineffective), learning science relies on empirical research and evidence-based strategies to optimize memory, understanding, and skill acquisition.

---

## 🧠 Core Principles of Learning Science

When reading the books in this section, you will see several recurring, evidence-based cognitive strategies:

1. **Retrieval Practice**: Actively recalling information from memory (testing yourself) rather than passively rereading or highlighting.
2. **Spaced Repetition**: Distributing review sessions over time to build durable, long-term neural connections.
3. **Interleaving**: Mixing up different topics or problem types during study rather than blocking them, which improves discrimination and problem-solving skills.
4. **Focused vs. Diffuse Modes**: Alternating between high concentration and relaxed, subconscious incubation to solve complex problems.
5. **Dual Coding**: Combining verbal descriptions with visual representations to enhance comprehension and recall.
6. **Active Engagement & Feedback**: Constantly testing hypothesis models, learning from errors, and consolidating them through high-quality sleep.
