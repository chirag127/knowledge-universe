# Small Teaching: Everyday Lessons from the Science of Learning

## Metadata
* **Authors**: James M. Lang
* **Published**: 2016 (2nd Edition 2021)
* **Edition**: 2nd (Jossey-Bass)
* **Genre**: Learning Science / Educational Practice / Classroom Methods
* **Description**: A pedagogical handbook that advocates for "small-ball" teaching — making incremental, research-based modifications to lectures, course designs, and communications rather than massive overhauls. The book breaks these micro-interventions into three core domains: Knowledge, Understanding, and Inspiration.
* **Companion Books**: *Powerful Teaching* (Agarwal & Bain), *Make It Stick* (Brown et al.), *How We Learn* (Carey)

## Should You Read the Full Book?

**Read the full book if** you are an educator (high school or college level) looking for a highly structured, classroom-oriented playbook for incremental course optimization. The book's primary strength is its structural organization: each chapter is divided into a review of cognitive theory, a detailed profile of "small teaching" in action, and a bulleted list of immediate, concrete models for the classroom (e.g., how to spend the first 5 minutes of a class, how to write quiz questions, and how to structure mid-term feedback). It is highly actionable for teachers who want to improve student outcomes without grading extra work or rebuilding their syllabi from scratch.

**Use the summaries instead if** you are a student, self-directed learner, or professional looking to apply learning science to your own life. Lang’s writing is heavily focused on classroom scenarios: managing large lecture halls, grading exams, designing online slide decks, and motivating uncooperative students. If you do not need tips on how to run a university classroom and simply want the core cognitive strategies (like predicting, interleaving, self-explaining, and emotional cues) to accelerate your own learning, these summaries isolate the actionable principles without the school-system packaging.

> **Verdict**: *Read the full book if you are a college professor or high school teacher looking for a practical, step-by-step framework to optimize your classroom instructions. Use the summaries if you want a direct guide to apply these micro-adjustments to your own study habits.*
