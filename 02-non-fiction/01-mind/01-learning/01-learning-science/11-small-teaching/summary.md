# Book Summary: Small Teaching: Everyday Lessons from the Science of Learning

## Overview
Lang argues that making massive structural overhauls to learning programs is often counter-productive. Instead, incorporating small, five-to-ten-minute cognitive exercises—such as asking students to predict answers, utilizing quick opening retrieval prompts, interleaving different skills, making visual connections, and cultivating academic belonging—aligns perfectly with brain biochemistry to spark superior long-term understanding.

---

### Core Learning Takeaways
* **Pillar 1**: A brilliant, highly practical guide detailing how minor, incremental cognitive changes in structure, presentation, and preparation can result in massive, durable learning gains.
* **Pillar 2**: Real-world application of memory, consolidation, and practice.
* **Pillar 3**: Overcoming study illusions through active, effortful retrieval.
