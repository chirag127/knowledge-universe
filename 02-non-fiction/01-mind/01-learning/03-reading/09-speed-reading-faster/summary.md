## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Speed reading is not about rushing through text but about training your brain and eyes to work together efficiently. By understanding how the eye and brain process written information — using peripheral vision, reading in meaningful chunks, moving eyes in optimal patterns, and engaging both brain hemispheres — anyone can double their reading speed while improving comprehension and retention. The key is systematic practice with immediate feedback: test your current speed, apply targeted techniques, measure improvement, and build a personalized system that works for your specific reading needs.

## 🧠 Key Concepts Glossary

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **Fixation** | The pause your eyes make to capture text; speed readers reduce fixation count |
| **Saccade** | The rapid eye movement between fixations; faster saccades = faster reading |
| **Regression** | Unnecessary backward eye movement; a primary speed limiter |
| **Chunking** | Reading words in meaningful groups rather than individually |
| **Peripheral Vision (Reading)** | Using side vision to capture words beyond the focal point |
| **Hemispheric Integration** | Coordinating left-brain (logic, language) and right-brain (pattern, image) processing |
| **NLP (Neuro-Linguistic Programming)** | Psychological approach used for anchoring resourceful reading states |
| **Key Information Identification** | Rapidly distinguishing essential content from filler |
| **Brain Dominance (Reading)** | Whether analytical or gestalt processing dominates your reading style |
| **Pattern Recognition** | The brain's ability to recognize word shapes without letter-by-letter decoding |
| **Subvocalization Reduction** | Minimizing the inner voice that "speaks" each word while reading |
| **Eye Span** | The number of words captured in a single fixation |
| **Meta-Guiding** | Using a pointer (finger, pen) to guide eye movement rhythmically |
| **Critical Analysis** | Evaluating text importance and reliability while reading |
