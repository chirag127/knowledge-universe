### About the Author

**Peter Kump** was the former National Director of Education for Evelyn Wood
Reading Dynamics, one of the most famous speed reading programs in the world.
For over 20 years, he taught rapid reading to thousands of individuals,
including business professionals, students, and executives. He lived and worked
in New York City.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- **Most structured speed reading program available** — 36 specific lessons with
  clear daily goals
- **Drill-based approach** is proven effective for skill acquisition
- **Emphasizes flexibility** — not just reading fast, but knowing WHEN to read
  fast
- **Realistic claims** — promises 2-4x improvement, not magical 10,000+ WPM
- **No gimmicks** — straightforward, practical, no "brain training" pseudoscience
- **Excellent for busy professionals** — 20 minutes/day fits most schedules
- **Comprehensive** — covers newspapers, mail, books, magazines, study reading

### Weaknesses / Criticism
- **Dated examples** — references to newspapers and physical mail feel antiquated
  in the digital age
- **No digital reading advice** — focuses entirely on print materials; no guidance
  for screens, e-readers, or web browsing
- **Repetitive** — some drills feel very similar across lessons
- **Requires strong self-discipline** — no built-in accountability
- **Hand-movement emphasis** may feel awkward or unnecessary to some readers
  (Butler and others argue it is not essential)
- **Limited comprehension theory** — more mechanical than cognitive

### Who Should Read This
- **Highly recommended for**: Self-disciplined learners who thrive on structured
  programs; professionals drowning in reading material; students wanting to
  reduce study time
- **Less suitable for**: Readers who dislike rigid drills; those looking for
  cognitive/neuroscience explanations; digital-only readers
- **Best approach**: Commit to 36 days, one lesson per day. Do ALL drills. Track
  every result. Continue maintenance drills after completion.

### Key Quotes
- "Rapid reading is a skill, not a concept."
- "A good reader is a flexible reader."
- "Your hand is your built-in reading accelerator."
- "Don't read just for its own sake — read with purpose."
- "The goal is not speed alone, but speed with comprehension."

### Connections to Evelyn Wood Reading Dynamics
Kump was the National Director of Education for Evelyn Wood Reading Dynamics,
a pioneering speed reading program from the 1950s. This book distills and
improves upon the Wood method, adding:
- More structured lesson progression (6 groups of 6)
- Greater emphasis on comprehension techniques
- Practical applications for specific materials
- Self-paced vs. classroom instruction
