### About the Author

**David Butler** is a speed reading instructor and researcher who developed the
right-brain approach after struggling with traditional speed reading methods.
At age 49, after years of reading frustration, he discovered that conventional
speed reading techniques were missing the comprehension component. He developed
his methodology independently and later licensed it to a speed reading company.
This is his first and primary book on the subject.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- **Comprehension-first philosophy** is a genuinely novel and needed
  corrective in the speed reading genre
- **Phrase-highlighting format** is unique — no other speed reading book uses
  this technique
- **20 practice passages** from classic literature make practice enjoyable
- **Debunks myths** that many speed reading books promote (e.g., eliminating
  subvocalization entirely, 10,000+ WPM claims)
- **Excellent for skeptics** who have tried other methods and found comprehension
  suffered
- **Personal, relatable tone** — the author's own struggles lend credibility

### Weaknesses / Criticism
- **Self-published** — some readers note the book could benefit from
  professional editing
- **Repetitive** in places; some chapters could be condensed
- **Scientific claims** about "right brain vs. left brain" are simplified;
  modern neuroscience shows the brain works more holistically
- **Less structured** than Kump's or Buzan's books — fewer drills and progress
  tracking tools
- **Limited application** to technical/dense material; the method works best
  with narrative and general non-fiction
- **No standardized tests** — progress is self-assessed

### Who Should Read This
- **Highly recommended for**: Readers who feel traditional speed reading
  sacrificed their comprehension; visual learners; those who enjoy reading
  classic literature
- **Less suitable for**: Those wanting a structured drill-based program with
  strict progress metrics; readers of highly technical material
- **Best approach**: Read the instructional chapters (1-14), then practice
  with the exercises (15-20) repeatedly over weeks

### Key Quotes
- "Reading happens in your brain, not your eyes."
- "Don't just speed read. Speed comprehend!"
- "The goal is not to read more words per minute, but to understand more ideas
  per minute."
- "When you focus on comprehension, speed takes care of itself."
