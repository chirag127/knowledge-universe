### About the Author

**Jonathan A. Levi** is a serial entrepreneur, keynote speaker, podcaster, and
bestselling author. After struggling as a "slow learner" with suspected ADHD,
he discovered accelerated learning techniques and taught them to over 220,000
students across 205 countries through his SuperLearner methodology. He hosts the
award-winning SuperHuman Academy podcast and has been featured in The Wall Street
Journal, Inc., BusinessInsider, and LifeHacker UK. He lives in Tel Aviv, Israel.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- **Accessible and engaging** — reads like a conversation, not a textbook
- **Solid synthesis** of proven techniques (Memory Palace, spaced repetition,
  SQ3R) into one coherent system
- **Good for beginners** who have never encountered accelerated learning
  techniques
- **Personal story** is compelling and builds trust
- **Actionable** — each chapter ends with specific next steps
- **Community aspect** — connects readers to online resources and community

### Weaknesses / Criticism
- **Self-promotion heavy** — frequently pushes the paid SuperLearner course
- **Lacks depth** — serious students of memory/speed reading will find the
  techniques covered superficially
- **Not original** — most techniques are available in other books (Foer,
  Lorayne, Adler) presented in more depth
- **Repetitive** in places; could be half the length
- **Oversimplified** "neuroscience" — some claims about the brain are
  overstated
- **Weak on speed reading** compared to dedicated books (Buzan, Butler, Kump)
- **Mixed reviews** — many 5-star ratings from beginners; critical reviews from
  experienced learners

### Who Should Read This
- **Highly recommended for**: Complete beginners to accelerated learning;
  busy professionals wanting a quick overview; fans of the SuperLearner
  podcast/course
- **Less suitable for**: Advanced readers who already know Memory Palace,
  spaced repetition, and SQ3R; those seeking rigorous scientific depth
- **Best approach**: Read quickly (it is designed for fast reading), implement
  the memory techniques, then supplement with dedicated books on each topic
