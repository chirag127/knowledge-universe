**Verdict**: Debbie Drum's *Read Better Faster* is the shortest and most controversial book in this category. Its core insight — using text-to-speech audio alongside visual reading (the "BuzzRead" method) — is genuinely useful for some readers, particularly auditory learners. However, the book is thin on substance, heavy on self-promotion, and lacks the structured practice regimen of its competitors. Best viewed as a supplement to a more comprehensive speed reading program.

## 💡 The Big Idea in One Paragraph

Traditional speed reading techniques (skimming, chunking, eliminating subvocalization) don't work for everyone because they fight against how your brain naturally processes language. Instead, use the BuzzRead method: play an audiobook or text-to-speech audio at 2-3x normal speed while following along visually with the text. This dual-input (visual + auditory) approach forces your brain to process faster, eliminates subvocalization naturally, improves focus, and creates stronger memory traces through multisensory encoding. You're not learning to "speed read" — you're learning to read at the speed of accelerated speech.

- Adults retain these inefficient habits
- Subvocalization is natural, not the enemy

### 2. Learning Styles & Self-Awareness (Assessment)
- Auditory, visual, and kinesthetic learning modalities
- Most speed reading techniques favor visual learners
- Auditory learners have been underserved by existing methods
- **Chapters**: 3

### 3. The BuzzRead Method (Core Technique)
- Simultaneous audio + visual reading
- Setting up text-to-speech at accelerated speeds
- Training the brain to process at speech speed

### 4. Memory & Retention Systems (Support)
