### About the Author

**Tony Buzan** (1942–2022) was a British author and educational consultant. He
is best known for inventing Mind Maps®, a widely used visual thinking tool. He
authored over 100 books on the brain, learning, memory, and creativity, and
advised corporations and Olympic athletes globally.

## 📝 Personal Notes

### Strengths
- Self-contained course with 7 graded reading tests to track progress
- Integrates Mind Mapping with speed reading — a unique combination
- Addresses root causes (subvocalization, regression) systematically
- Vocabulary chapters (16-18) provide a structured vocabulary building system
- The "Knowledge File" concept is presciently analog but insightful for the
  pre-digital era
- Progress charts and graphs give clear visual feedback of improvement

### Weaknesses / Criticism
- Some claims about reading speeds (1000+ WPM with full comprehension) are
  contested by reading research
- Mind Map material dominates — feels like a Buzan product pitch at times
- Self-published feel in later editions; limited professional editing
- Exercises require a significant time commitment
- The "brain-friendly" framing can feel gimmicky
- Less rigorous than Kump's *Breakthrough Rapid Reading* in terms of drills

### Who Should Read This
- **Highly recommended for**: Visual learners, Mind Map enthusiasts,
  self-paced learners who want a structured course
- **Less suitable for**: Readers wanting pure theory or research-driven
  approaches; those who prefer drill-based practice
- **Best for**: Beginners to speed reading who want a holistic,
  well-rounded introduction

### Key Quotes
- "The brain is capable of far more than you think."
- "Reading is not a single skill but a complex set of skills."
- "Your eyes are the window to your brain — and they can be trained."
